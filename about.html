My Journey Into CS

My path into computer science began unexpectedly as a kid building HTML websites, then completely forgetting about coding until I took Introduction to Digital Technology. That class reignited something in me—I started enjoying the backend of code throughout high school, working with JavaScript and eventually solving problems with Python.

I'm graduating this fall with what I believe is a truly diverse background. I've intentionally worked across a wide range of disciplines—from product design and user research to back-end development and site reliability engineering. This isn't a lack of focus; it's a deliberate strategy to gain a comprehensive, 360-degree understanding of the entire software development lifecycle, from initial concept all the way to a deployed and performant application.

For example, I started by learning about user research and how analysis can lead to identifying which features help users. Then I moved into the design of those features—specifically front-end design—and eventually into implementation, where I discovered how efficiency matters when thinking about application performance management.

My internships, particularly at Raymond James, have been incredibly influential. There, I gained firsthand experience with real-world problems like threading issues and the critical importance of code efficiency. That experience solidified my belief that the best code isn't just functional—it's also robust and reliable.

Academically, my concentration in both artificial intelligence and people has given me a unique perspective. My research on how human decision-making can be replicated by computers has taught me to think about the deeper, often unquantifiable nuances behind complex problems. I believe this ability to connect the technical with the human element is what allows me to consistently think outside the box and bring new, innovative ideas to the table.

Ultimately, I'm passionate about what I do, and I'm always eager to learn, adapt, and bring a holistic perspective to any project I work on.
